Preparation

Natural disasters can strike at any moment, but creating a disaster plan can help to minimize confusion and can leave you and your loved ones reassured that you have a prepared plan when disaster does strike.

Creating a Disaster Plan

  • Sign up for any severe weather alerts within your region.
  • Agree upon a given method of digital communication (for example, A family group chat).
  • Decide on an agreed meeting point for everyone within the household.
  • Prepare an escape route for your hosehold and for your neighborhood, make sure to keep alternate routes in mind as certain natural disasters can lead to certain routes to be blocked.
  • Make sure to educate all adults and teens on how to shut off electrical and water lines if there's a leak or electrical short. Also make sure to keep tools within reach.
  • Consider First Aid and or CPR training and certification.
  • If you have pets in the household, make sure to coordinate who will take care of each pet and be sure to know how to act accordingly to given circumstances. Make sure each pet has ID tags.

A well thought out disaster kit is essential to disaster plan. Make sure to have a disaster proof case and to keep your kit in an agreed spot.

Here are some items to consider:

  • A water supply: Make sure to have a suffcient amount of water for every individual in your house to last at least 3 days
  • Non-persible food: such as canned goods and protien bars
  • Can opener
  • Flashlights, lanterns, batteries
  • Clothing and blankets
  • First aid kit
  • Water-proof matches
  • Radio
  • Sanitation Utencils: toilet paper, soap, moist towellettes, trashbags, and disinfectant

Here are some items that might be more specific to your circumstances:

  • Baby food, diapers, bottles
  • Pet food
  • Medicine
  • Extra eyeglasses or contacts
  • Wrenches or pliers

Having an emergency fund can help you avoid going into debt or cause any other inconviences.

Help prepare children to plan for and cope with disasters

Take time to prepare your children for disasters by talking to them about:

  • The disaster plan: Take some time to get them to get to know the important parts of the plan, such as escape routes, and what their roles are in the plan
  • Who to contact when seprated from the parents
  • What to do when away from home during an emergency
